Who we are?

MSAU is the Muslim Students Association of Uganda that was formed in 1962 with the main objective of producing muslim students who are visionary, responsible, responsive.

Our Vision

MSAU's vision is “is to become a knowledgeable, responsible and responsive Muslim students' population practicing Islam in total, participating in and contributing to the progress of the Muslim Ummah, in Uganda and the world at large.”

Our Mission

MSAU seeks “to be a vibrant association that mobilizes and sensitizes the Muslim students to lead an Islamic life, participate in and contribute towards programmes aimed at moving the Muslim community forward through promotion of education, good leadership and moral uprightness, using the Qur'an and Sunnah as supreme sources of guidance.

Our Objectives

To promote Academic Excellency among Muslim students in Uganda.
To promote Moral Uprightness among Muslim students.
To foster Unity among Muslim students in Uganda.
To Unite and Coordinate Muslim students activities within Uganda.
To encourage Muslim parents educate their children.
To safeguard Muslim students Rights in various schools/institutions in Uganda.
To train Muslim students in Leadership and Management skills.
To amalgamate with any Muslim organization pursuing similar causes.
To promote the Social Welfare of Muslim students.
To uphold the international dignity of Islam within and outside Uganda.
To formulate and defend the Interests of Muslim students in Uganda.
To represent Ugandan Muslim students on all regional, national and international forums.
To raise funds that will promote the cause of Muslim students in Uganda.
To translate Islamic literature into local languages for easy propagation of Islam.
To publish and distribute Islamic books, journals, magazines, newsletters, periodicals that facilitate propagation of Islam.

Membership Eligibility

Membership of MSAU shall be open to all Muslim students associations within and outside Uganda regardless of race or tribe.
All Muslim students associations in Uganda are deemed to be affiliated to MSAU.
All Muslim students associations in Uganda in the level of primary schools to higher institutions of learning shall be members.
All members shall have full voting rights and shall be eligible for election to offices of MSAU or affiliated students associations.
The Division shall keep an up to date register of all registered Muslim Students' Associations (MSAs) in the division.

Rights and Obligations of Members

Attending general meetings.
Proposing motions and moving resolutions.
Nominating and seconding new office bearers in the general meeting.
Participating in general activities of MSAU.
Electing and sending delegates to the MSAU Delegates Conference.
Muslim Students' Associations are free to conduct activities and inform the Division leadership.
Any Division level disputes can be arbitrated by the DEC Kampala.
Any member is free to notify the General Secretary that he intends to move a motion at a meeting that a particular member be expelled.

Cessation of Membership

Any member or association for any reason cease to subscribe to the memorandum and Articles of Association of MSAU shall ipso facto cease to be a member of MSAU. When a motion to expel a member from the association is supported by at least 2/3 of the members present in the meeting, the member concerned shall forthwith cease to be a member.
Where a member has lost membership, his/her name shall be deregistered.
Members may also upon completion of their studies cease their membership.
